# FINN-WILSON. Content Engine
**Version 1.0 · June 2026 · Goal: build sector reputation by December 2026**

---

## The logic

You have 2,908 connections from the L1 campaign. They accepted because the sector description matched. Most of them don't yet know what you think, what you've built, or why your view is worth following.

Content solves that. Every post is a signal to the right people that you're the practitioner voice in their feed — not a vendor, not a consultant, not an analyst. The operator who has already run the programme they're about to start.

The measure of success is not followers. It's the right people — tier-1 contacts, future clients, NZ/emerging market operators — DM-ing you because something you posted crystallised a problem they're living.

---

## What you're building toward

**90 days:** establish a recognisable point of view. The audience should be able to predict the lens you'll apply to any piece of sector news.

**6 months:** your content is cited in conversations ("I saw you post about X..."). Network replies reference your posts. Inbound DMs from people who found you through content.

**12 months:** the content-to-consultancy pipeline is working. The tools get traffic from posts. The SRA has warm leads before you pitch.

## The four formats — and their jobs

Every post maps to exactly one format. No format = no post.

| Format | Stream | Job | Signal that triggers it |
|--------|--------|-----|------------------------|
| **Teardown** | From the Field | Operator credibility | A real number, observation, or failure you witnessed |
| **Thesis Post** | Reframe | Differentiation | Industry news you can apply your operating-model lens to |
| **Framework** | Scale Lessons | Sell consultancy thinking | A principle, lesson, or decision structure worth naming |
| **Map + Tool Pull** | Quick Map | Feed the funnel | A decision people get wrong + your tool solves it |

**Cadence rule:** Max 1 Map + Tool Pull per 4 posts. The other 3 earn the trust that makes the 4th convert.

## Weekly rhythm

**Non-negotiable time blocks:**

| Block | When | Duration | What happens |
|-------|------|----------|-------------|
| Signal scan | Monday morning | 15 min | Review inbox signals, news, HeyReach threads — pull 2–3 post ideas |
| Post 1 | Tuesday | 30 min | Write, review, publish |
| Engagement | Daily | 10 min | 1–2 comments on others' posts (not "great post" — add a specific observation) |
| Post 2 | Thursday | 30 min | Write, review, publish |
| Feedback review | Friday (during network scan) | 15 min | Log what landed, why, what to do differently |

**Total time per week: ~2.5 hours.** Everything else compounds this — it doesn't replace it.

## The signal-to-post pipeline

This is where the content engine links to the insight gathering system.

### Signal sources (in priority order)

1. **HeyReach conversations** — the most underused source. Matthijs van den Heuvel's question about shared HDC is a post. Kristofer Fröjd's BESS thesis is a post. Sarah Papamichalis's Shell Recharge intel is a post. The inbox is a market research tool.

2. **market-insight-graph** — when a Signal or Insight node is logged, ask: does this fit a format? If yes, add it to the content queue. The Activation step of the pipeline is often "write a Thesis Post."

3. **LinkedIn feed** — what are the 5–10 accounts in your sector posting? What are they getting wrong, oversimplifying, or ignoring? That gap is a Thesis Post.

4. **Industry news** — Decade Energy funding, Shell Recharge divestment, Osprey's £110M facility, Monta partnership with evyve. Each one is raw material.

5. **Prologis work** — field observations, aggregated and anonymised. "A 40-site portfolio I reviewed this quarter..." is more compelling than any LinkedIn opinion piece.

### Signal triage (15 min, Monday)

For each signal, ask three questions:
1. Can I say something specific? (not "interesting trend" — a number, a named failure, a mechanism)
2. Which format does this fit?
3. Is it timely, or can it wait?

Signals that don't pass question 1 get discarded. Specificity is the whole point.

### From signal to draft (30 min)

The fastest way to a publishable post:
1. Write the hook first — one line, specific tension or number
2. Follow the format structure (see references/linkedin-formats.md)
3. Read it back using the two tests: chatbot test + operator test
4. Cut anything that sounds like someone else could have written it

## Feedback loop — how you learn fast

The goal is to close the loop within 7 days of publishing. Here's what to track:

### What to measure (Friday, 10 min)

For each post this week, log:
- **Format** used
- **Topic** / signal that triggered it
- **Engagement type**: comments vs. reactions vs. reposts
- **Who engaged**: were they the right people? (tier-1/2 contacts, decision-makers, target orgs)
- **DMs generated**: did anyone message you directly because of this post?
- **Profile visits spike**: check LinkedIn Creator Analytics the day after publishing

Do NOT optimise for raw impressions or like counts. A post that gets 3 comments from Philip Valarino, James Moat, and a logistics director is better than one that gets 500 reactions from a general audience.

### What to do with the data

After 4 weeks (the first rotation), answer these:
- Which format got the most right-people engagement?
- Which topics drew in tier-1/2 contacts?
- Which posts generated DMs or new conversations?
- What did the comments tell you that you didn't already know? (These go back into market-insight-graph as Signals)

This is the feedback to the insight engine. The content doesn't just publish — it gathers intelligence that sharpens the next cycle.

### Green flags (do more of this)
- Comments from operators at logistics/fleet/energy companies
- DMs from people asking how you solved a specific problem
- Tier-1 contacts reacting or commenting
- Posts referenced in conversation threads ("I saw you posted about X...")

### Red flags (recalibrate)
- High impressions, zero comments
- Comments only from other consultants / content creators (echo chamber)
- Engagement entirely from people you already know
- Thesis posts getting pushback from operators (means the claim wasn't grounded enough)

## Linking to the insight engine

When content feedback generates a signal, it goes back into market-insight-graph:

```
Comment: "We see the same thing on our fleet — the grid connection delay is killing ROI"
→ Evidence node: "Grid connection delay as ROI killer — 3 operator confirmations"
→ Signal: "Grid capacity is the true bottleneck, not hardware or policy"
→ Insight: "Operators systematically underestimate grid lead times in business cases"
→ Next content activation: Framework post — "5 things operators get wrong in depot charging business cases"
```

This is how the system compounds. Each post generates feedback that sharpens the next post.

## Hard rules

1. **Never write without a signal.** If you're staring at a blank page, go back to the inbox, the graph, or the news.
2. **Specificity over breadth.** A post about one thing works better than a post about everything.
3. **No format = no post.** If you can't identify which of the four jobs a post serves, don't publish it.
4. **The Teardown requires real numbers.** Aggregated, anonymised — but real. Don't invent.
5. **Post 2 days, comment 5 days.** The comments build relationships faster than the posts in the early months.

## Content tracking

Track posts in ClickUp (Content Pipeline list, Stream field per format). Minimum fields per post:
- Format (Teardown / Thesis / Framework / Map+Tool)
- Signal source (HeyReach / graph / news / field)
- Date published
- Engagement tier (A = right people; B = general; C = echo chamber)
- DMs generated (Y/N + who)
- Feed back to graph (Y/N + what)

A monthly log of 8 posts with these fields will show you the pattern within 60 days.

## The first 90 days

**Month 1 (June–July):** Establish the thesis. Every post is either a Teardown or a Thesis Post. Prove the operating-model lens with real observations. Let the Framework posts come later once the credibility is established.

**Month 2 (July–August):** Introduce Frameworks. Now that the audience has seen you're grounded in reality, the frameworks feel earned rather than generic.

**Month 3 (August–September):** First Map + Tool Pull cycle. By now the ERA tool and TCO calculator have an audience that knows why they'd need them. The CTA lands on warm ground.
