# Source Strategy

## Goal

Collect market material from places where people reveal pain, urgency, confusion, desire, language, buying intent, and competitor movement.

The system should prioritize signal-rich sources over generic news volume.

## Source Buckets

### 1. Audience Voice

Sources where the target audience speaks in their own words.

Examples:

- Reddit threads
- LinkedIn comments
- YouTube comments
- community posts
- forum discussions
- customer interviews
- sales notes
- support conversations
- product reviews

Best for:

- pain points
- objections
- desired outcomes
- language patterns
- urgency
- emotional texture

### 2. Competitor And Peer Signals

Sources that reveal market positioning and offer movement.

Examples:

- competitor homepages
- pricing pages
- product updates
- newsletters
- founder posts
- hiring pages
- case studies
- webinars

Best for:

- messaging shifts
- category framing
- offer design
- positioning patterns
- market convergence

### 3. Market And Industry Sources

Sources that reveal macro movement and expert framing.

Examples:

- analyst reports
- industry newsletters
- podcasts
- conference talks
- whitepapers
- research papers
- regulation updates

Best for:

- trends
- category shifts
- why-now arguments
- external validation

### 4. Search And Demand Signals

Sources that reveal what people are actively looking for.

Examples:

- search trends
- keyword data
- search console
- autocomplete patterns
- people-also-ask questions
- forum search results

Best for:

- demand patterns
- recurring questions
- content opportunities
- topic language

### 5. Platform And Content Signals

Sources that reveal what narratives are resonating.

Examples:

- high-performing posts
- creator feeds
- comments on viral content
- newsletters with strong engagement
- podcast episode topics
- content performance data

Best for:

- content angles
- topic fatigue
- emerging language
- audience resonance

### 6. Owned Thinking And Feedback

Sources generated by the user and their market.

Examples:

- personal notes
- strategy docs
- published posts
- replies and comments
- calls
- email threads
- offer experiments
- content analytics

Best for:

- validating strategy
- creating original theses
- closing the loop between publishing and market response

## Collection Rules

1. Start narrow and high-signal.
2. Preserve source metadata.
3. Capture original language when useful.
4. Avoid collecting more than can be reviewed.
5. Prefer official APIs, feeds, exports, and manual imports.
6. Respect platform terms, paywalls, and privacy.

## Initial Source Registry

The first registry should include:

- 5 audience communities
- 5 competitors or peers
- 5 newsletters or blogs
- 3 recurring search topics
- 3 owned feedback sources

## Review Cadence

Daily:

- capture new evidence
- flag unusual signals

Weekly:

- review new signals
- merge duplicates
- create or update insights
- choose content opportunities

Monthly:

- review insight clusters
- update strategic bets
- archive stale insights
- identify research gaps
