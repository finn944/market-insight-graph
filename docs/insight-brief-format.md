# Insight Brief Format

## Purpose

Insight briefs turn clusters of evidence and signals into clear, usable strategic thinking.

They should be concise, source-backed, and connected to action.

## Brief Template

```text
Title
A short, specific name for the insight.

Thesis
The core observation in one or two sentences.

Evidence
The strongest source-backed signals that support the thesis.

Why It Matters
The strategic implication.

Audience
Who this insight matters to.

Market Movement
What appears to be changing.

Confidence
Low, medium, or high, with a short rationale.

Contradictions
Evidence or signals that challenge the thesis.

Content Angles
Possible posts, essays, hooks, narratives, or contrarian takes.

Offer Implications
What this could mean for products, services, packages, or positioning.

Strategic Action
What to consider doing next.

Open Questions
What needs more evidence.
```

## Example

```text
Title
Founders Want Sharper Strategy Without Heavyweight Planning

Thesis
Independent founders and operators want clearer strategic direction, but many resist traditional planning processes because they feel slow, corporate, or abstract.

Evidence
- Repeated complaints about planning frameworks being too theoretical
- Strong engagement with lightweight decision-making posts
- Competitors emphasizing speed, clarity, and action over formal strategy

Why It Matters
There may be demand for strategy tools and services that feel practical, creative, and alive rather than corporate.

Audience
Independent founders, consultants, creators, and small-team operators.

Market Movement
The market may be shifting from static planning documents toward lightweight strategic operating systems.

Confidence
Medium. Evidence is recurring but still needs more direct customer-language support.

Contradictions
Some buyers still want formal deliverables when budgets are larger or stakeholders are involved.

Content Angles
- Strategy should feel like a working instrument, not a PDF.
- Why smart founders avoid strategy work.
- The difference between clarity and planning theater.

Offer Implications
A lightweight strategic clarity sprint may resonate better than a broad strategy consulting package.

Strategic Action
Test content around lightweight strategy and collect audience responses.

Open Questions
Which language resonates more: strategy system, clarity sprint, operating narrative, or market map?
```

## Quality Bar

A good insight brief should:

- say something specific
- point to evidence
- name the audience
- explain why it matters
- include uncertainty
- suggest action
- connect to other nodes in the graph

A weak brief usually:

- summarizes generic trends
- lacks evidence
- uses broad audience labels
- has no contradiction or confidence note
- does not change any action
